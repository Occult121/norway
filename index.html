TRONDHEUM

Trondheim is Norway’s third-largest city and its former Viking-age capital, founded in 997 AD. Located in central Norway along the Trondheim Fjord, it is a major hub for technology, education, and history. Often called the "Technology Capital of Norway," it is home to the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), which gives the city a young, vibrant student-led energy

Skudeneshavn

a picturesque coastal town located on the southernmost tip of the island of Karmøy in Rogaland, Norway. Often referred to as "Norway’s Summer Town," it is famous for Old Skudeneshavn (Gamle Skudeneshavn), which contains one of Europe's best-preserved collections of 19th-century white wooden houses.

Fredrikstad

a historic coastal city in southeastern Norway, located at the mouth of the river Glomma. Founded in 1567 by King Frederick II, it is most famous for its Old Town (Gamlebyen), which is recognized as Northern Europe’s best-preserved fortified town. The city effectively blends its 17th-century heritage with a modern, creative atmosphere, often referred to as "Plankebyen" (The Plank Town) due to its historical timber industry.
